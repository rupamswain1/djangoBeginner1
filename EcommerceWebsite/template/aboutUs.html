Django Ecomm

Django Ecomm.is an Indian Demo company based in Bhubaneswar, Odisha, which focuses on learning Django. It is considered one of the Big Five companies in the U.S. information technology industry, along with Google, Apple, Microsoft, and Facebook. The company has been referred to as "one of the most influential economic and cultural forces in the world", as well as the world's most valuable brand. Django Ecomm was founded by Rupam Swain in Bhubaneswar, Odisha in 14 Nov 2020 while learning Django. The company started as an online marketplace for books but expanded to sell electronics, software, video games, apparel, furniture, food, toys, and jewelry. In 2015, Django Ecomm surpassed Walmart as the most valuable retailer in the United States by market capitalization. In 2017, Django Ecomm acquired Whole Foods Market for US$13.4 billion, which substantially increased its footprint as a physical retailer. In 2018, Bezos announced that its two-day delivery service, Django Ecomm is known for its disruption of well-established industries through technological innovation and mass scale. It is the world's largest online marketplace, AI assistant provider, live-streaming platform and cloud computing platform as measured by revenue and market capitalization. Django Ecomm is the largest Internet company by revenue in the world. It is the second largest private employer in the United States and one of the world's most valuable companies. Django Ecomm distributes downloads and streaming of video, music, and audiobooks through its Prime Video, Django Ecomm Music, Twitch, and Audible subsidiaries. Django Ecomm also has a publishing arm, Django Ecomm Publishing, a film and television studio, Django Ecomm Studios, and a cloud computing subsidiary, Django Ecomm Web Services. It produces consumer electronics including Kindle e-readers, Fire tablets, Fire TV, and Echo devices. Its acquisitions over the years include Ring, Twitch, Whole Foods Market, and IMDb. The company has been criticized for various practices including technological surveillance overreach, a hyper-competitive and demanding work culture, tax avoidance, and for being anti-competitive.
